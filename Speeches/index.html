The future of GNOME and internationalization

Kenneth Rohde Christiansen


     <kenneth@gnu.org>
    

Abstract

GNOME is really moving along nicely, and we have reached a state where it is almost ready for general consumption. GNOME 2.0 brings lots of improvements regarding usability and accessibility; and also brings major advantages for localization, including Pango.

Pango doesn't solve everything for us all by itself. First of all we rely heavily on gettext and intltool. We also rely on a very strict release schedule for handling translations. So far it has been possible to translate GNOME into a variety of languages, but we have not yet done to make GNOME act professionally in these languages.

There are major problems with our current way of releasing and having a good localized GNOME desktop. People download new updates with such tools as Red Carpet, and suddenly their favorite program is no longer 100% translated but only 80%percnt;, and what does this “sort descending” mean?

Big problems like these are what keep GNOME from being really usable in non-English languages.

In this speech I shall talk about our current tools and release methods. I will introduce developers to some of the hidden but very useful features that intltool has.

As the end of the speech, I shall suggest a detailed idea of how to improve some of these problems. After that, we can discuss alternative ideas and figure out the future of GNOME and localization.

Problems we have today

A couple of years ago we were having a lot of problems with translation, since a lot of new file formats like .glade and .server.in showed up. Together with Maciej and Darin, I wrote intltool and we solved most of these problems.

During this work we noticed that it was a huge help to centralize all kinds of translations into the .po files. This made it possible to write status scripts that showed us exactly how well our language was supported, and also gave people a hint on when they had to start working again :)

There were still problems left. The most notable one was that people didn't mail the <gnome-i18n@gnome.org> mailing list to announce that they were about to make new releases. This has improved a lot, but it is still not perfect.

We also noticed that people often left out files from the POTFILES.in file, so I wrote a --maintain option to check for this.

Over the last year intltool has envolved and added features like this to help maintaining translations work. Unfortunately only very few people know how to take advantage of these features, and only a few people want to use intltool, so I have been thinking about looking into the possibility of integrading intltool with gettext. Whether this is a good idea or not, I am not certain.
